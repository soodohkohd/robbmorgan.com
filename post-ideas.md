# Post Ideas

Successors to **"Climbing the IC Ladder Is a Gamble."** — the post that connected
because it named something everyone has lived but rarely says out loud, had a
clear villain (broken org design) and a clear ask ("fix the ladder"), and hit
pay/title/dignity at once. None of these are AI-flavored. All of them are
things people are already arguing about in Slack DMs.

---

## 1. "Your Performance Review Wasn't About You."

The calibration meeting decided your rating before your manager wrote a word.
People in a room you can't enter, fitting you onto a curve someone invented,
often with no memory of your work. Everyone who's been "exceeds-meets'd" down
to a 3% raise knows this in their bones.

- **Villain:** forced distribution
- **Ask:** kill the curve, rate the work
- **Why it lands:** universally experienced, rarely said out loud

---

## 2. "Unlimited PTO Is a Pay Cut."

Companies switched to "unlimited" so they didn't have to pay out balances at
termination — and average usage *dropped* when they did. The headline says
freedom; the spreadsheet says payroll savings. This is a wallet issue, which
is why it'll land.

- **Closing line writes itself:** *if it's unlimited, take eight weeks. See what happens.*
- **Why it lands:** the data point is concrete and the betrayal is specific
- **Sleeper pick** — most shareable of the eight

---

## 3. "The Layoff Wasn't About Performance."

Survivors are told the "low performers" were cut. They weren't. Tenure, comp
band, and team headcount targets were cut. The myth exists to keep the
survivors grateful and the departed quiet.

- **Why it lands:** validates what every laid-off senior engineer already suspects
- **Emotional register:** angry, in a useful way

---

## 4. "Return to Office Was Never About Productivity."

It was about real-estate write-downs, attrition-by-attrition (cheaper than
severance), and middle-management anxiety about what they actually do all day.
Pick a side and commit — half-measures (3 days, anchor days, "flexible") are
the worst of all worlds.

- **Will generate angry comments from VPs** — that's the point
- **Why it lands:** everyone has a take, almost no one believes the official story

---

## 5. "Your Senior Title Means Nothing Anymore."

Senior at 25. Staff at 28. Principal at 30. Director of Engineering managing
four people. Title inflation has compressed twenty years of career signal
into five, and the market knows — which is why your "Staff" offer at the new
place comes in at Senior money.

- **Hits ego AND wallet**
- **Natural companion to the IC Ladder post**

---

## 6. "The Manager Tax."

You have a manager. Your manager has a manager. That manager has a director.
The director has a senior director. The senior director has a VP. The VP has
an SVP. There are seven people between you and a decision, and six of them
have never written code that runs in production.

- **Why it lands:** everyone in a big company is already grumbling about this
- **Job to do:** name it

---

## 7. "The Hiring Process Has Become an Insult."

Seven rounds. A take-home that takes a weekend. A system-design interview
with a 26-year-old who's never operated the system at scale. Then radio
silence. We've built a hiring funnel that selects for people who have time
to play the game, not people who can do the job.

- **Pairs with:** "Your Resume Never Made It to a Human." (already published)
- **Positioning:** the next chapter of that argument

---

## 8. "Stop Calling It a Reorg."

Annual box-shuffling on the org chart is performance theater. It signals
motion without solving the underlying problem (which is usually that the
strategy is incoherent or the wrong VP got promoted). Reorgs reset every
team's relationships, kill in-flight work, and buy leadership twelve months
before anyone can ask what changed.

- **Why it lands:** everyone has lived through 3+ of these
- **Tone:** dry, exhausted, knowing
